Business insurance: what you really pay for and what stays uncovered

Business insurance is not just “fire insurance”. It’s a protection structure for property, liability, and (when needed) business interruption. The most common mistake is declaring sums insured roughly and chasing the lowest premium only.

Underinsurance may reduce claim payments proportionally in a major loss. Overinsurance increases premium without any extra benefit beyond the actual damage. Deductibles can lower the premium, but you must know the out-of-pocket amount you accept in each claim.
