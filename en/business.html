Liability insurance: why “cheapest premium” often backfires

Liability insurance pays when a third party suffers bodily injury or property damage as a result of your activity or premises. The real differences between policies are not only the premium, but the limit, exclusions, deductibles and whether the wording properly reflects what you actually do.

The most common mistake is choosing a low limit just to reduce cost while the exposure is higher (public access, premises, products, employees). You don’t need exaggeration—you need correct matching. With the information above, we can propose options with clear terms.
